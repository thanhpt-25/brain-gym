# Exam Prep with Study Plans

> Learn how to use CertGym's adaptive study plans to prepare efficiently for your certification exam.

---

## What Is a Study Plan?

A **study plan** is a personalized roadmap that schedules exam topics across your available study time. Instead of you manually deciding what to study each day, CertGym analyzes:

- **Your knowledge level** in each exam domain (via practice tests)
- **Your schedule** (when you can study)
- **The exam date** (how much time you have)

Then it creates a day-by-day plan that maximizes your readiness.

---

## Getting Started: Create Your Study Plan

### Step 1: Set Your Exam Date

1. Go to `/exam-prep`
2. Click "Create Study Plan"
3. Select your certification exam (e.g., "AWS Solutions Architect Associate")
4. Enter your **target exam date**
   - Recommended: at least 4-6 weeks away
   - If sooner, plan assumes intensive daily study

### Step 2: Assess Your Current Knowledge

CertGym will ask you to take a **diagnostic quiz**:

- 30-40 questions across all exam domains
- Takes 45 minutes to 1 hour
- Gives baseline knowledge level (Beginner / Intermediate / Advanced)

**Why**: The study plan adjusts based on your level. If you're already advanced in certain domains, the plan skips those and focuses on weaknesses.

### Step 3: Set Your Study Availability

Tell CertGym how much you can study:

- **Daily study time** (e.g., 1 hour, 2 hours, 3+ hours)
- **Days per week** (e.g., 5 days = weekdays only)
- **Blackout dates** (e.g., vacation, important work projects)

Example:

- Target exam date: August 15, 2026
- Current date: June 1, 2026
- Available study time: 2 hours per day, 6 days per week
- → Plan distributes ~240 hours of study across 11 weeks

### Step 4: Review Your Plan

CertGym generates a calendar view:

- **Week-by-week breakdown**: Which domains to focus on each week
- **Daily tasks**: Specific topics, flashcard reviews, practice questions
- **Milestones**: Checkpoint exams every 2 weeks to track progress
- **Flexibility**: Reorder topics or adjust dates if needed

## How the Plan Works

### Daily Study Flow

Each day, your plan shows:

1. **New Content** (20 minutes)
   - Learn 1-2 new topics via video + notes
   - Example: "VPC Basics" or "IAM Policies"

2. **Flashcard Review** (15 minutes)
   - Review concepts using spaced-repetition (SM-2 algorithm)
   - App reminds you what you're likely to forget
   - Mark cards as learned, reviewing, or struggling

3. **Practice Questions** (45 minutes)
   - Topic-specific practice (not full exams yet)
   - Receive explanations for wrong answers
   - See domain breakdown (which topics you're weak in)

4. **Optional: Deeper Dive** (15+ minutes)
   - Advanced reading or video on topics you're struggling with
   - Optional, but recommended if you're behind

### Milestone Exams

Every 2 weeks, the plan schedules a **checkpoint exam**:

- Full practice exam (same length/difficulty as real exam)
- Timed (no cheating by peeking at answers)
- Scored and analyzed
- Shows your current readiness %

**How readiness % works**:

- Baseline after diagnostic: 25-60% (depending on your starting level)
- Target for exam day: 85%+ (statistically likely to pass)
- Plan adjusts based on milestone scores

Example progression:

- Week 1 milestone: 35% readiness
- Week 3 milestone: 48% readiness
- Week 5 milestone: 62% readiness
- Week 7 milestone: 75% readiness
- Week 9 milestone: 88% readiness ✓ Ready to sit the exam

## Following Your Plan

### Staying on Track

**✓ Do this:**

- Complete daily tasks in order (they're sequenced logically)
- Finish flashcard reviews before moving to next topic
- Take checkpoint exams on scheduled dates
- Mark topics as "struggling" if you need extra help

**✗ Don't do this:**

- Jump around topics randomly (spaced-repetition breaks)
- Skip flashcard reviews (they reinforce memory)
- Cram all practice on the last week (you need time to consolidate)
- Ignore weak domain areas

### Adjusting Your Plan

If your schedule changes:

1. **Falling behind?** → Add 30 minutes/day or extend exam date
2. **Ahead of schedule?** → Take checkpoint exam early and graduate
3. **Need to skip days?** → Mark dates as "vacation" and plan reschedules
4. **Topic too hard?** → Request "extended focus" (adds 2-3 extra days) for that domain

To adjust, click "Edit Plan" anytime (changes propagate to future tasks).

## Study Plan Features

### 1. Spaced-Repetition Flashcards

CertGym uses the **SM-2 algorithm** to decide when to show you cards:

- **New card**: Shown immediately after you create it
- **Learning**: Shown 1 day later, then 3 days later
- **Review**: Shown 7-30 days later depending on how well you know it
- **Mastered**: Shown once a month as a refresher

**Result**: You spend time on cards you're forgetting, not ones you know cold.

### 2. Domain Breakdown

After each practice session or checkpoint exam, you see:

- **Weakest domains**: Where to focus (sorted by accuracy)
- **Strongest domains**: Your confidence areas
- **Time spent per domain**: Is your study time proportional to difficulty?

Example report:

```
AWS EC2 (65% accuracy) → needs focus
AWS S3 (82% accuracy) → OK
AWS RDS (58% accuracy) → needs focus
Networking (88% accuracy) → strong
```

### 3. Streak Tracking

Daily study counts as a streak:

- Complete today's tasks → +1 day
- Skip a day → streak resets
- Leaderboard shows top study streaks (competitive motivation)
- Milestones: 7-day, 30-day, 60-day streak badges

### 4. Study Groups & Notes

- **Share your plan** with classmates (read-only view of your schedule)
- **See others' progress** (who's ahead, who's struggling)
- **Annotate topics** with your own notes ("Remember: X is important")
- **Mark questions** for discussion in study group

## Common Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Falling Behind

**Your milestone was 65%, target was 70%.**

Options:

1. **Add daily time** (2 hours → 2.5 hours)
   - Compresses timeline but more intensive
2. **Extend exam date** (Aug 15 → Sept 1)
   - Gives more time without increasing daily load
3. **Focus on weak domains** (skip strong domains, focus on weak ones)
   - Risky; could cause other areas to decay

**Recommendation**: Extend by 2-3 weeks rather than burning out. Better to be well-prepared than exhausted.

### Scenario 2: Way Ahead of Schedule

**Your milestone was 80%, target was 70%. It's week 5 of 8.**

Options:

1. **Take the checkpoint exam early** (validate you're truly ready)
2. **Deep-dive into weak areas** (turn 80% into 90%+)
3. **Sit the real exam early** (if your testing center allows)

**Recommendation**: Aim for 85-90% readiness, then sit the exam. Don't overstudy; diminishing returns after 85%.

### Scenario 3: Balancing Work & Study

**You have a big project at work that week.**

1. Mark that week as "limited availability" (reduce daily task load)
2. Or mark specific dates as "vacation"
3. Plan adjusts by compressing study into other weeks

**Tip**: Let the plan reschedule rather than cramming. A missed week + light schedule is better than 2 weeks of overwork.

## Exam Day Checklist

### Days Before the Exam

- [ ] **Review weak domains** one more time
- [ ] **Take a practice exam** 2-3 days before (full test in exam-like conditions)
- [ ] **Get good sleep** the night before (no cramming)
- [ ] **Relax**: You've prepared; trust your study plan

### Morning of the Exam

- [ ] **Eat breakfast** (stable blood sugar helps focus)
- [ ] **Review test-taking tips** (1-page summary in your study plan)
- [ ] **Arrive early** (15 min before to settle in)
- [ ] **Check ID & testing rules** (if in-person exam)

### During the Exam

- [ ] **Read each question carefully** (don't rush)
- [ ] **Flag hard questions** and skip them (come back if time)
- [ ] **Manage time**: 1 min per question is typical
- [ ] **Don't second-guess yourself** (first instinct is often right)

## Tips for Success

1. **Start early** (4-6 weeks gives time to absorb)
2. **Study daily** (consistency beats cramming)
3. **Use flashcards** (spaced-rep is proven effective)
4. **Take practice exams** seriously (simulate real exam conditions)
5. **Join a study group** (external accountability helps)
6. **Review weak domains** (focus time where you need it most)
7. **Sleep well** (memory consolidates during sleep)
8. **Stay healthy** (exercise reduces stress, improves focus)

## FAQ

**Q: How long should a study plan take?**
A: 4-8 weeks depending on starting level. Advanced learners might need 4 weeks; beginners might need 10+ weeks.

**Q: Can I study faster and sit the exam sooner?**
A: Yes. If your readiness score is 85%+, you're ready. Sit the exam whenever your testing center allows.

**Q: What if I miss a day?**
A: No penalty. Just mark it as "vacation" and the plan reschedules. One missed day won't derail you.

**Q: Should I do other study materials besides CertGym?**
A: Optional. CertGym covers all exam domains. Extra resources help, but aren't required if you follow the plan.

**Q: How is readiness % calculated?**
A: Based on practice test scores, flashcard mastery %, and weak domain analysis. 85%+ is the pass threshold.

**Q: Can I retake the plan if I fail the exam?**
A: Yes. Create a new plan, focus on domains where you scored low, and retry.
