# Burnout Detection User Guide

## What is Burnout Detection?

CertGym's Burnout Detection feature monitors your study patterns in real-time to help you maintain a healthy balance between ambitious learning goals and sustainable study habits. When your studying shows signs of exhaustion—such as declining scores, extended sessions without breaks, or increasing error rates—the system alerts you with personalized recommendations.

### Why It Matters

**Study burnout** is real. It happens when you push too hard for too long without adequate recovery time. The consequences include:
- Declining exam performance (grades go down despite more studying)
- Reduced information retention (you forget what you studied)
- Increased frustration and loss of motivation
- Sleep disruption and physical fatigue
- Long-term loss of interest in the subject

CertGym's Burnout Detection helps you catch these patterns early so you can course-correct before they impact your certification goals.

## How Burnout is Detected

The system continuously monitors four key study patterns:

### 1. **Score Decline** (35% of burnout score)
- Your recent exam scores are trending downward
- Example: Scored 85% → 78% → 72% in your last three attempts
- **Healthy Pattern**: Stable or improving scores

### 2. **Time Allocation** (25% of burnout score)
- Your study sessions are getting longer without breaks
- Example: 2hr → 3hr → 4hr+ continuous sessions
- **Healthy Pattern**: 45-90 minute sessions with 15-minute breaks

### 3. **Attempt Frequency** (20% of burnout score)
- You're taking exams much more frequently than before
- Example: 2 attempts/day → 5 attempts/day
- **Healthy Pattern**: Consistent, sustainable attempt frequency

### 4. **Error Rate** (20% of burnout score)
- You're making more mistakes on questions you previously got right
- Example: Previously scored 90% on AWS Security, now scoring 60%
- **Healthy Pattern**: Consistent accuracy or improving on weak topics

## Understanding Severity Levels

### 🟢 Low Severity (Score < 0.50)
**What it means**: Your study patterns are healthy and sustainable.

**What you should do**:
- Continue with your current study pace
- Maintain the same number of attempts and session length
- Keep up your current balance of study and rest

### 🟡 Medium Severity (Score 0.50-0.69)
**What it means**: Some warning signs are appearing. Not critical yet, but worth monitoring.

**What you should do**:
- **Pace your studying**: Reduce from 5 attempts/day to 3, from 3-hour sessions to 90 minutes
- **Take regular breaks**: 15-minute break after every 45 minutes of studying
- **Review your study routine**: Are you preparing effectively, or just going through the motions?
- **Diversify topics**: Don't focus on just one domain; spread topics evenly

### 🟠 High Severity (Score 0.70-0.84)
**What it means**: Significant burnout signals detected. Your study efficiency is likely declining.

**What you should do**:
- **Take a break**: 15-20 minute break immediately
- **Chat with AI Coach**: Your coach can analyze your patterns and suggest a recovery plan
- **Reduce attempt frequency**: Cut back to 1-2 attempts per day for the next 3-5 days
- **Focus on quality over quantity**: Review past mistakes rather than taking more exams
- **Check your health**: Are you sleeping 7-8 hours? Eating well?

### 🔴 Critical Severity (Score ≥ 0.85)
**What it means**: Urgent intervention needed. Your study patterns show severe burnout risk.

**What you should do RIGHT NOW**:
1. **Take a 30-minute break** - Step away from studying immediately
2. **Chat with your AI Coach** - Priority intervention recommended
3. **Rest for the next 24 hours** - No exams, no studying
4. **Assess your situation**:
   - Are you stressed about certification deadline?
   - Do you have other life stressors?
   - Is this certification worth your health?
5. **Create a recovery plan** with your coach (starting tomorrow)

## Taking Action

### When You Get a Burnout Alert

**Step 1: Understand the Alert**
- Hover over the severity level to see detailed explanation
- Look at which signals are contributing most (score decline vs. frequency)
- This is diagnostic information, not judgment

**Step 2: Take Immediate Action**
- **Low**: Nothing required. Stay the course.
- **Medium**: Take a 15-min break, review your study plan
- **High**: Take a 20-min break, then chat with coach
- **Critical**: Take a 30-min break, definitely chat with coach

**Step 3: Make Adjustments**
- **Reduce attempt frequency**: Aim for 1-2 exams/day instead of 5+
- **Shorten session length**: 45-90 minute sessions with breaks
- **Switch to coach sessions**: Use AI coach for targeted guidance
- **Try flashcard review**: Lower-stakes way to practice

**Step 4: Track Recovery**
- The system will re-evaluate in 24 hours
- You should see improvement within 2-3 days of taking breaks
- Your scores will likely go UP after proper rest (counterintuitive but true!)

### Dismissing Alerts

You can dismiss a burnout alert by clicking the **Dismiss** button. However:
- Dismissing doesn't disable the monitoring (we keep watching)
- We won't show alerts again for 24 hours after dismissal
- **Pro tip**: Don't dismiss without taking action; just taking the recommended action is better

## FAQ

### "Why did I get a critical alert when I feel fine?"

The system looks at objective behavioral data (scores, frequency, session length), not how you feel. This is intentional because:
- People often underestimate their own exhaustion level
- By the time you *feel* burned out, your learning efficiency is already down
- The system catches it early, when recovery is fastest

Take the alert seriously even if you feel okay. Trust the data!

### "How often is burnout recalculated?"

The system updates after every exam attempt and during background processing (every few hours). So:
- After you take a break, the next exam will show improvement
- The system is always monitoring, even if you don't see alerts
- Severity can go down as quickly as it goes up (good news!)

### "I'm on a tight deadline for my certification exam. Should I ignore burnout alerts?"

No! Here's why:
- **Burnout reduces learning retention** - studying more while burned out is counterproductive
- **Rest improves performance** - taking 2-3 days off often results in higher exam scores
- **Better to delay slightly and succeed** than push hard and fail the certification
- **Coach can help with an alternative timeline** - they specialize in recovery plans

### "Can I adjust the burnout sensitivity?"

Not currently, but this might be a future feature. For now:
- You can dismiss individual alerts if you believe they're false
- Feedback on false alerts helps us improve the system
- [Contact support](mailto:support@certgym.com) if you're getting repeated alerts you think are wrong

### "Does burnout detection affect my score or certification?"

No, not at all! Burnout detection is purely informational:
- It doesn't count toward your score
- It doesn't affect certification eligibility
- It's just a helpful tool to optimize your learning
- Dismissing alerts doesn't impact anything

### "What if I need to study hard because of a real deadline?"

We understand! That's exactly why the coach exists. They can:
- Create a time-boxed intensive study plan
- Suggest the most efficient topics to study first
- Help you avoid common deadline panic mistakes
- Monitor you more closely during the sprint

**Chat with your coach about your timeline.** They'll help you create a sustainable intensive study plan that minimizes burnout while meeting your deadline.

### "Is my burnout data private?"

Yes! Here's our commitment:
- Burnout scores are stored securely in your private profile
- Only you (and coaching staff if authorized) can see them
- They're not shared in leaderboards or organization analytics
- Data is encrypted at rest and in transit

## Best Practices for Sustainable Learning

Based on research and our community's experience, here are habits that prevent burnout:

### Daily
- ✅ Study in 45-90 minute focused sessions
- ✅ Take 15-minute breaks between sessions
- ✅ Drink water, eat healthy snacks
- ✅ Move around (walk, stretch) during breaks
- ✅ Study different domains (don't focus on one all day)

### Weekly
- ✅ Have at least 1 full day (24+ hours) with no studying
- ✅ Schedule one "fun" study session (topic you enjoy)
- ✅ Review progress and celebrate wins
- ✅ Adjust your study plan based on weak areas
- ✅ Check sleep quality (7-8 hours/night is essential)

### During Intensive Study Periods
- ✅ Use spaced repetition (flashcards) instead of just exams
- ✅ Chat with your AI coach 2-3 times per week
- ✅ Prioritize high-value topics
- ✅ Take extra breaks on high-burnout-risk days
- ✅ Set a clear end date for intensive study mode

## When to Seek Additional Help

If you're experiencing burnout alerts AND:
- **Persistent low mood or depression** → Contact a mental health professional
- **Sleep disruption lasting > 1 week** → See a doctor
- **Significant anxiety about certification** → Talk to your coach about timeline
- **Conflict between study and life responsibilities** → Review your commitment to this certification
- **Physical symptoms (headaches, tension)** → Rest and consult a healthcare provider

Your health is more important than any certification. CertGym is a tool to support your learning, not a reason to suffer.
